How this library was made

Photographs

Every marker is photographed in the field; original files are archived untouched. Web versions (thumbnails and display images) are generated with Pillow.

The enhanced view

The "flip" view that pulls worn carving and lettering out of the stone is computed with OpenCV: contrast-limited adaptive histogram equalization (CLAHE) applied to the lightness channel only — so the slate keeps its true color — plus a gentle unsharp mask. Nothing is drawn or invented; it only redistributes the contrast already present in the photograph.

Outlines

Silhouettes are traced automatically: the stone is separated from its background with rembg (the U²-Net segmentation model), and OpenCV traces and simplifies the contour into a small SVG. Every outline is reviewed by the curator against its photograph before publication; imperfect traces are rejected.

Virtual raking light

The 💡 viewer estimates a relief map of each stone's surface with Depth Anything V2 (monocular depth estimation), then relights it in your browser with WebGL — mimicking the grazing light gravestone scholars use in the field. It is an interpretation of a single photograph, not a measurement.

Shape space

The shape-space view applies geometric morphometrics to the approved silhouettes: each outline is resampled to equally spaced points, normalized, and analyzed by principal components — placing every stone in a space where distance means shape difference. Decade-average silhouettes and "similar shapes" suggestions come from the same analysis.

Inscriptions & translations

Transcriptions are drafted with the help of Claude (an AI assistant) reading the enhanced photographs — many of these stones are inscribed in Welsh — then verified and corrected by the curator letter-by-letter against the stone. Translations from the Welsh are provided in the notes.

The site

Maps: MapLibre GL JS with OpenFreeMap tiles (© OpenStreetMap contributors). Charts: Chart.js. Type: Jost & Source Sans 3. Hosted on GitHub Pages with images on Cloudflare R2. A companion to The Story of Slate.
